## Purpose

This portfolio documents governance-oriented AI work through verifiable projects, public research, technical implementation, and external validation.

The objective is not to present claims.

The objective is to present evidence.

Core themes include:

* governance observability
* admissibility
* authority preservation
* deterministic evaluation
* runtime governance
* evidence-based system design

---

## Mission

Design and study AI systems where:

* authority remains explicit
* evaluation remains bounded
* governance remains observable
* evidence remains reconstructable
* autonomy remains constrained by governance

Core principle:

> Demonstration is stronger than declaration.

---

## Governance Philosophy

### Evidence Before Assertion

Governance claims should be independently verifiable.

### Demonstration Before Declaration

The existence of a control is documentation.

The exercise of a control is evidence.

### Governance Before Autonomy

Autonomy should be considered only after governance conditions are demonstrated.

### Authority Preservation

Evaluation, recommendation and analysis must remain separated from authority and consequence ownership.

### Human Accountability

Humans retain responsibility for governance decisions and outcomes.
